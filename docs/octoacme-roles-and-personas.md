# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Engineers

### Role Summary
QA Engineers validate quality and acceptance criteria throughout development. They design and execute test plans, identify defects, and ensure software meets functional and non-functional requirements before release.

### Responsibilities
- Design comprehensive test plans and test cases
- Execute manual and automated tests
- Identify, document, and track defects
- Validate acceptance criteria are met
- Perform regression testing and smoke tests
- Collaborate with developers on test automation strategy
- Sign off on quality gates before releases

### Goals
- Ensure high-quality deliverables with minimal defects
- Prevent regressions through comprehensive test coverage
- Reduce time-to-detection for defects
- Enable continuous quality feedback

### Typical Communication
- Daily standup participation and blocker escalation
- Test reports and quality metrics dashboards
- Defect triage meetings with developers
- Release readiness sign-off documentation

---

## Engineering Leads / Technical Leads

### Role Summary
Engineering Leads provide technical direction, architecture guidance, and mentorship. They make key technical decisions, ensure code quality standards, and bridge communication between engineering teams and stakeholders.

### Responsibilities
- Define technical architecture and design patterns
- Review critical code changes and technical designs
- Mentor developers and conduct technical interviews
- Make build vs. buy decisions and technology choices
- Ensure technical debt is managed and addressed
- Resolve technical blockers and escalations
- Coordinate with other technical leads on dependencies

### Goals
- Maintain high technical standards and code quality
- Enable team productivity and technical excellence
- Balance technical debt with feature delivery
- Foster engineering growth and learning culture

### Typical Communication
- Architecture review meetings
- Technical design documents and RFCs
- One-on-ones with engineers for mentorship
- Cross-team technical sync meetings
- Critical code review comments and approvals

---

## Stakeholders / Sponsors

### Role Summary
Stakeholders and Sponsors provide business direction, funding approval, and strategic alignment. They define business objectives, approve scope changes, and ensure projects deliver expected business value.

### Responsibilities
- Define business objectives and success criteria
- Approve project charters and major scope changes
- Provide resources and remove organizational blockers
- Review progress and provide strategic guidance
- Ensure alignment with business priorities
- Make go/no-go decisions at decision gates

### Goals
- Maximize business value and ROI
- Ensure strategic alignment across initiatives
- Maintain visibility into project health and risks
- Enable teams with necessary resources and support

### Typical Communication
- Monthly stakeholder briefings and status reports
- Decision gate reviews and approvals
- Escalation discussions for critical issues
- Quarterly business reviews and roadmap planning

---

## Designers / UX Specialists

### Role Summary
Designers and UX Specialists focus on user experience, visual design, and usability. They conduct user research, create design systems, and ensure products are intuitive and delightful to use.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and prototypes
- Develop and maintain design systems
- Collaborate with product and engineering on user flows
- Validate designs through user feedback and A/B testing
- Ensure accessibility and inclusive design standards
- Define and track UX metrics

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive and accessible user experiences
- Reduce user friction and improve satisfaction scores
- Maintain design consistency across products
- Validate design decisions with user research data

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- User research findings and personas
- Prototype demos and usability test results
- Design specifications and handoff documentation
- Collaboration with developers during implementation
